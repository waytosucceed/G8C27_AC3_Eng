Subject, Predicate and their types


Key concepts

The subject is a word or a set of words that refers to the person or thing that performs an action or about whom or which something is stated. It must be a noun or a noun equivalent and can contain any number of words. Subjects are of three types: Simple, Complete and Compound. Subjects have pre-modifiers before them and post-modifiers after them.

The predicate is a word or collection of words that expresses what is said about the subject or what the subject does. It must include at least one finite verb, stated or perceived. It can include any verb, helping verb or phrases. Predicates are of three types: Simple, Complete and Compound. Complete predicates contain, verb phrase, objects, modifiers or complements
